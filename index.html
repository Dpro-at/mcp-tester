What is MCP?

Model Context Protocol (MCP) is an open protocol that standardizes how applications provide context to Large Language Models (LLMs). Think of MCP as the "USB-C for AI"—it lets you connect your AI models to any data source or tool in a secure, flexible, and vendor-neutral way. Learn more on the official MCP website.

  • Build agents and complex workflows on top of LLMs
  • Integrate with a growing list of pre-built tools and data sources
  • Switch between LLM providers easily
  • Follow best practices for security and data privacy

General Architecture: MCP uses a client-server model where hosts (like IDEs or AI tools) connect to MCP servers that expose resources, tools, and prompts. This enables seamless integration between your data, your tools, and your AI models.
